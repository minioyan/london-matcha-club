The people behind LMC

We are Kenji & Clara.

A Japanese craftsman and a London designer, sharing a quiet obsession with the way a single bowl of matcha can change the pace of a day.

We opened London Matcha Club not to build a brand, but because we couldn't find the place we were looking for. Five years later, we're still brewing slowly, still making the clothes we want to wear, still learning from every bowl.

Our story

We met in East London, over bad coffee and a conversation that lasted four hours.

"Kenji said he could make something better than what we were drinking. He was right." — Clara

Kenji grew up in Kyoto, where tea is not a beverage but a practice. After fifteen years in London working as a graphic designer, he found himself increasingly drawn back to the rituals of his upbringing — the quiet, the slowness, the way a single bowl could reframe an entire morning.

Clara, born and raised in Hackney, had spent her career designing interiors that felt like exhales. When the two of them began talking about opening a space together, they were describing the same place without knowing it.

London Matcha Club opened quietly, with no announcement and a handwritten sign. That was five years ago. The bonsai in the window has been there since day one.

Our philosophy

Calm is not an aesthetic.
It's a decision.

Everything we do — the matcha we source, the clothes we make, the way we arrange the room — comes from the same impulse: to make space for the unhurried. We're not minimalists out of taste. We're minimalists out of necessity.

Sourcing

Direct from producers

Every matcha we serve comes from farms we have visited personally. We don't use distributors. We don't compromise on shade time.

Making

Natural fibres only

The LMC clothing line uses organic cotton and linen, produced in small batches in Portugal and Japan. Nothing synthetic. Nothing wasteful.

Presence

A real place, online

We wanted LMC to feel as tangible on a screen as it does in the room. The same light, the same pace, the same sense of something unhurried.
